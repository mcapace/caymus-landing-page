The Caymus Point of View

A philosophy shaped by fifty years of listening to the people who drink our wines.

No. 1 – Challenging the Status Quo

"There's room for both expert voices and everyday enjoyment. Critics offer insight, but the people who drink the wine define its meaning."

No. 2 – Consumer-First Thinking

"You don't need a glossary to know what tastes good. That's the wine we aim to make, no translation required."

No. 3 – Real-World Validation

"A packed tasting room and national accounts moving serious volume says more to us than a 94-point score ever could."

No. 4 – Made to Be Enjoyed

"We've always made wines for sharing—something that brings people together, not just wines to admire."

No. 5 – Gatekeeping vs. Hospitality

"Expertise matters. But when influence turns into ego, wine stops being welcoming. Our role is to help people enjoy what's in the glass, not tell them what they're supposed to like."

No. 6 – Sommelier Culture

"Too many wine lists are built to impress other sommeliers, not the guests meant to enjoy the wine."

No. 7 – Why We Make Wine

"We're in business to make wines people choose again and again. Sales, repeat purchase, and loyalty are the clearest signals that we're doing our job."

No. 8 – Pride in Popularity

"Sometimes the industry treats popularity like it's a flaw. To us, it means we're doing something right."

No. 9 – Consistency Over Time

"Very few wine brands endure. Most rise, peak, and fade. We've learned that staying relevant means listening, showing restraint, and the discipline to keep doing what people love, year after year."

No. 10 – The Future Belongs to the People

"The world changes, tastes evolve, and new generations come to the table on their own terms. If wine stays welcoming and focused on enjoyment, it stays relevant."
